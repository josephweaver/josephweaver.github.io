# Research Context

This compact governs the use of generative AI tools in research,
software development, technical writing, and data engineering activities
associated with my graduate research and the SMART AI Fellowship.

The primary principle of this compact is that generative AI systems may
assist with exploration, iteration, and implementation, but scientific
understanding, verification, and responsibility remain entirely human
obligations.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Appropriate Uses of AI

Generative AI tools may be used for:

- brainstorming research ideas and hypotheses,
- exploring modeling or software strategies,
- drafting prototype code or workflows,
- technical reference assistance such as debugging or syntax
  clarification,
- summarization and organizational support,
- grammar and clarity improvements for writing,
- automation of repetitive engineering tasks,
- development acceleration for ETL and data-processing infrastructure.

AI-generated outputs are treated as suggestions requiring human
evaluation and verification, not authoritative results.

All substantive research conclusions, interpretations, analyses, and
final technical decisions remain under direct human control.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Human Verification and Ownership Requirements

Any code, mathematical derivation, statistical procedure, or scientific
conclusion used in research output must satisfy the following
conditions:

- I must understand the underlying logic and assumptions.
- I must be able to explain and defend the result independently.
- Core methods must be reproducible without reliance on AI interaction.
- Outputs must be validated through testing, robustness checks, or
  independent reasoning.
- AI-generated code may not be incorporated blindly into research
  pipelines.

The use of AI does not replace the researcher's responsibility for
correctness, reproducibility, or ethical conduct.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Restrictions and Inappropriate Uses

The following uses are considered inappropriate:

- submitting AI-generated work without review or understanding,
- allowing AI systems to make unverified scientific conclusions,
- using AI to fabricate citations, analyses, or results,
- incorporating opaque code or methods that cannot be independently
  explained,
- uploading confidential, proprietary, or restricted research materials
  to unapproved platforms,
- representing AI-generated content as independent human scholarship.

Generative AI tools may not substitute for critical thinking,
methodological understanding, or independent verification.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Transparency and Reproducibility

When appropriate, AI usage will be documented at a level consistent with
disciplinary norms and advisor expectations.

Research outputs should remain reproducible and understandable without
requiring access to the original AI interaction history.

AI assistance is viewed as analogous to an accelerated technical
reference or prototyping aid, not as an autonomous research
collaborator.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Approved Platforms and Compliance

Only institutionally approved AI platforms and accounts will be used for
university-related research activities, consistent with Michigan State
University and College of Natural Science guidance.

Care will be taken to avoid disclosure of confidential, proprietary, or
restricted information.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Guiding Principle

The intended workflow is:

> AI-assisted exploration → human understanding → independent
> verification → reproducible result

The purpose of AI use is to accelerate learning, engineering, and
iteration while preserving human ownership, accountability, and
scientific integrity.
